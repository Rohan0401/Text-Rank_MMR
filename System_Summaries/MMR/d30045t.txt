Exxon Corp. and Mobil Corp. have held discussions about combining their business operations, a person involved in the talks said Wednesday.If the companies were to merge, it would create the largest U.S. company in terms of revenue.He added that the heads of both companies, Lee Raymond, the chairman of Exxon, which is based in Irving, Texas, and Lucio Noto, the chairman, president and chief executive of Mobil, which is based in Fairfax, Va., are different personalities.``It will not be easy,'' he said of combining the two far-flung companies, which have vast networks of refineries and gas stations that overlap in the United States and Europe.
