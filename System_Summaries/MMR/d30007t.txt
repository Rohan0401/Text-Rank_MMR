Rebels attacked a village in western Uganda and killed six civilians before soldiers drove them off, a military spokesman said Thursday.The attack occurred overnight Wednesday in the village of Chiondo near Kasese,  kilometers ( miles) west of the capital Kampala, said the spokesman, who identified himself only as Lt. Majera.Majera said the attackers were believed to be rebels of the Allied Democratic Forces who operate from bases in the foothills of the Rwenzori Mountains that straddle the Uganda-Congolese border.Muruli said the LRA had been planning to bomb several sites in the Ugandan capital, including the Parliament building, state-run Radio Uganda, unidentified embassies and other public sites, the newspaper said.
